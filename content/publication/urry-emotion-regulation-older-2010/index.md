abstract: Older age is normatively associated with losses in physical, cognitive,
  and social domains. Despite these losses, older adults often report higher levels
  of well-being than do younger adults. How can we explain this enhancement of well-being?
  In this article, we consider one possible explanation, namely, that older adults
  show enhanced emotion regulation. Specifically, we propose that older adults achieve
  well-being by selecting and optimizing particular emotion regulation processes to
  compensate for changes in internal and external resources. With this framework in
  mind, we suggest several directions for future research.
