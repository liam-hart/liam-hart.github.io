abstract: This article reports the development and validation of a scale to measure
  global life satisfaction, the Satisfaction With Life Scale (SWLS). Among the various
  components of subjective well-being, the SWLS is narrowly focused to assess global
  life satisfaction and does not tap related constructs such as positive affect or
  loneliness. The SWLS is shown to have favorable psychometric properties, including
  high internal consistency and high temporal reliability. Scores on the SWLS correlate
  moderately to highly with other measures of subjective well-being, and correlate
  predictably with specific personality characteristics. It is noted that the SWLS
  is Suited for use with different age groups, and other potential uses of the scale
  are discussed.
