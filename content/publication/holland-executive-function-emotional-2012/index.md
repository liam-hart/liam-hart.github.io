title: Executive Function and Emotional Focus in Autobiographical Memory Specificity
  in Older Adults
authors:
- Carol A. Holland
- Nathan Ridout
- Edward Walford
- Jennifer Geraghty
date: '2012-11-01'
publishDate: '2025-05-28T02:32:39.921960Z'
publication_types:
- article-journal
publication: '*Memory*'
doi: 10.1080/09658211.2012.703210
abstract: The current study examined the role of executive function in retrieval of
  specific autobiographical memories in older adults with regard to control of emotion
  during retrieval. Older and younger adults retrieved memories of specific events
  in response to emotionally positive, negative and neutral word cues. Contributions
  of inhibitory and updating elements of executive function to variance in autobiographical
  specificity were assessed to determine processes involved in the commonly found
  age-related reduction in specificity. A negative relationship between age and specificity
  was only found in retrieval to neutral cues. Alternative explanations of this age
  preservation of specificity of emotional recall are explored, within the context
  of control of emotion in the self-memory system and preserved emotional processing
  and positivity effect in older adults. The pattern of relationships suggests updating,
  rather than inhibition, as the source of age-related reduction in specificity, but
  that emotional processing (particularly of positively valenced memories) is not
  influenced by age-related variance in executive control. The tendency of older adults
  to focus on positive material may thus act as a buffer against detrimental effects
  of reduced executive function capacity on autobiographical retrieval, representing
  a possible target for interventions to improve specificity of autobiographical memory
  retrieval in older adults.
