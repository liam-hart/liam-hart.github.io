abstract: Psychological states such as thoughts and feelings are real. Brain states
  are real. The problem is that the two are not real in the same way, creating the
  mind---brain correspondence problem. In this article, I present a possible solution
  to this problem that involves two suggestions. First, complex psychological states
  such as emotion and cognition can be thought of as constructed events that can be
  causally reduced to a set of more basic, psychologically primitive ingredients that
  are more clearly respected by the brain. Second, complex psychological categories
  like emotion and cognition are the phenomena that require explanation in psychology,
  and, therefore, they cannot be abandoned by science. Describing the content and
  structure of these categories is a necessary and valuable scientific activity.
