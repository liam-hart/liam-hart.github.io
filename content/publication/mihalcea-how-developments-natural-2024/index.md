abstract: The ways people use language can reveal clues to their emotions, social
  behaviours, thinking styles, cultures and the worlds around them. In the past two
  decades, research at the intersection of social psychology and computer science
  has been developing tools to analyse natural language from written or spoken text
  to better understand social processes and behaviour. The goal of this Review is
  to provide a brief overview of the methods and data currently being used and to
  discuss the underlying meaning of what language analyses can reveal in comparison
  with more traditional methodologies such as surveys or hand-scored language samples.
