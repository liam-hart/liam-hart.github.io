title: The Role of Motivation in the Age-Related Positivity Effect in Autobiographical
  Memory
authors:
- Quinn Kennedy
- Mara Mather
- Laura L. Carstensen
date: '2004-03-01'
publishDate: '2025-05-28T02:32:40.226906Z'
publication_types:
- article-journal
publication: '*Psychological Science*'
doi: 10.1111/j.0956-7976.2004.01503011.x
abstract: This study reveals that older adults have a positivity effect in long-term
  autobiographical memory and that a positivity bias can be induced in younger adults
  by a heightened motivation to regulate current emotional well-being. Three hundred
  nuns, ages 47 to 102 years, recalled personal information originally reported 14
  years earlier. They did so under experimental conditions that repeatedly primed
  them to focus on their current emotional states or on their memory accuracy, or
  that provided no instructional focus (control condition). Both older control participants
  and participants who were focused on emotional states showed a tendency to remember
  the past more positively than they originally reported in 1987. In contrast, both
  younger control participants and participants who were focused on accuracy tended
  to remember the past more negatively than originally reported.
