abstract: Socioemotional selectivity theory (SST) is a theory of life-span development
  grounded in the uniquely human ability to monitor time. SST maintains that the approach
  of endings---whether due to aging or other endings such as geographic relocations
  and severe illness---elicits motivational changes in which emotionally meaningful
  goals are prioritized over exploration. Research guided by SST has informed preferences,
  social networks, and emotional experience and led to the discovery of the positivity
  effect in cognitive processing. This article, based on my 2015 Robert W. Kleemeier
  Award Lecture, describes the development of SST and its related program of empirical
  research.
