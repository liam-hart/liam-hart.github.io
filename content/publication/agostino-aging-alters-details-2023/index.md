---
title: Aging Alters the Details Recollected from Emotional Narratives
authors:
- Caterina Agostino
- Signy Sheldon
date: '2023-01-01'
publishDate: '2025-05-28T02:32:37.586333Z'
publication_types:
- article-journal
publication: '*Aging, Neuropsychology, and Cognition*'
doi: 10.1080/13825585.2021.1962792
abstract: Although it is known that aging impairs episodic memory, the precise effect
  of aging on emotional memory is not fully understood. In this study, younger and
  older adults listened to narratives that contained general and emotional (positive,
  negative, or neutral) details as they viewed related images. When participants later
  recalled the narratives, both age groups remembered more emotional details from
  the negative than the positive or neutral narratives. Interestingly, the enhanced
  recall for the negative narratives came with a reduced ability to remember the associated
  images for both younger and older adults. For all narrative types, older adults
  recalled a similar number of general but fewer emotional details than younger adults.
  Although there were no age-specific emotional effects, memory functioning of the
  older adults related to better recall of the positive narratives and associated
  images. These results provide insight into the similarities and differences in how
  younger and older adults encode and retrieve complex emotional memories.
