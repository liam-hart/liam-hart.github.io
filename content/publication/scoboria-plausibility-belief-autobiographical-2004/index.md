abstract: Abstract             Investigations of the recovery and falsification of
  childhood memories have used one construct in lieu of another. Autobiographical
  memories have typically not been distinguished from autobiographical beliefs, and
  researchers have assumed that plausibility and schematic knowledge measure identical
  constructs. We tested the hypothesis that plausibility, autobiographical belief,
  and autobiographical memory are nested constructs, such that memory implies belief
  and belief implies plausibility. Six hundred and eighty five students answered questions
  about these constructs in relation to ten possible childhood events. Analysis of
  item means, response probabilities and the frequency with which items followed the
  predicted order demonstrated that the predicted pattern was upheld in over 95% of
  cases. Results did not support the hypothesis that plausibility and script knowledge
  are significantly related. Copyright o̧pyright 2004 John Wiley & Sons, Ltd.
