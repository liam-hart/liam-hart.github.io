abstract: Current conceptualizations of childhood amnesia assume that there is a ``barrier''
  to remembering early experiences that must be overcome in order for one to begin
  to accumulate autobiographical memories. In contrast, we present a social-cultural-developmental
  perspective on the emergence of autobiographical memory. We first demonstrate the
  gradual emergence of autobiographical memories across the preschool years and then
  relate this developmental process to specific developments in language, narrative,
  and understanding of self and other that vary among individuals, as well as by culture
  and gender.
