  Younger and Older Adults

publication: '*Applied Cognitive Psychology*'
doi: 10.1002/acp.1641
abstract: Abstract             Groups of younger and older participants produced cultural
  life scripts by listing the seven most important life events and the expected timing
  of these events for a hypothetical person. They also produced the seven most important
  life story memories from their own lives. Cultural life scripts and life story memories
  were rated on valence. Scales on depression, satisfaction with life, and the centrality
  of an event for identity and the life story were administered. A stable cultural
  life script was found across the two generations, with a clear bump for positive
  events in adolescence and early adulthood. However, older adults produced a more
  realistic, less idealized life script than younger adults. The overlap between life
  script events and life story memories increased with age. Having a negative event
  central to one's life story and identity was related to less life satisfaction and,
  in the young group, higher depression scores. Copyright o̧pyright 2009 John Wiley
