abstract: Theories hold that autobiographical memory serves several broad functions
  (directive, self, and social). In the current study, items were derived from the
  theoretical literature to create the Thinking About Life Experiences (TALE) questionnaire
  to empirically assess these three functions. Participants (N = 167) completed the
  TALE. To examine convergent validity, they also rated their overall tendency to
  think about and to talk about the past and completed the Reminiscence Functions
  Scale (Webster, 1997). The results lend support to the existence of these theoretical
  functions, but also offer room for refinements in future thinking about both the
  breadth and specificity of the functions that autobiographical memory serves. [PUBLICATION
