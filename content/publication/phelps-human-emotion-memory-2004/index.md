abstract: The amygdala and hippocampal complex, two medial temporal lobe structures,
  are linked to two independent memory systems, each with unique characteristic functions.
  In emotional situations, these two systems interact in subtle but important ways.
  Specifically, the amygdala can modulate both the encoding and the storage of hippocampal-dependent
  memories. The hippocampal complex, by forming episodic representations of the emotional
  significance and interpretation of events, can influence the amygdala response when
  emotional stimuli are encountered. Although these are independent memory systems,
  they act in concert when emotion meets memory.
