---
title: 'Preserved Learning and Retention of Pattern-Analyzing Skill in Amnesia: Dissociation
  of Knowing How and Knowing That'
authors:
- Neal J. Cohen
- Larry R. Squire
date: '1980-10-01'
publishDate: '2025-05-28T02:32:38.515045Z'
publication_types:
- article-journal
publication: '*Science*'
doi: 10.1126/science.7414331
abstract: Amnesic patients acquired a mirror-reading skill at a rate equivalent to
  that of matched control subjects and retained it for at least 3 months. The results
  indicate that the class of preserved learning skills in amnesia is broader than
  previously reported. Amnesia seems to spare information that is based on rules or
  procedures, as contrasted with information that is data-based or declarative---\"knowing
  how\" rather than \"knowing that.\" The results support the hypothesis that such
  a distinction is honored by the nervous system.
