abstract: This paper briefly reviews the evidence for multistore theories of memory
  and points out some difficulties with the approach. An alternative framework for
  human memory research is then outlined in terms of depth or levels of processing.
  Some current data and arguments are reexamined in the light of this alternative
  framework and implications for further research considered.
