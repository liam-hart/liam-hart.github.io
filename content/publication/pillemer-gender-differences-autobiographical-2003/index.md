publication: '*Memory*'
doi: 10.1080/09658210244000117
abstract: Older adults provided oral life histories in a semi-structured interview
  format. The transcribed narratives were coded for the presence of specific, one-moment-in-time
  episodes. Participants differed systematically in the degree to which their narratives
  were marked by descriptions of specific events. Women's memory styles were markedly
  more specific or episodic than were men's styles. Participants' ratings of the ways
  that they use memory in daily life suggest that women place a greater value on purposeful
  reminiscence than do men.
