title: Explaining Change in Content of Life Narratives over Time
authors:

- Tilmann Habermas

publication: '*Memory*'
doi: 10.1080/09658211.2020.1761397
abstract: Aiming to further the understanding of stability and change of the life
  story, we investigated memory age and valence of life events as possible factors
  influencing life narrative stability. Additionally, we examined personal memories
  that 145 participants ranging in age from 16 to 69 years had told four years earlier
  but omitted from their current life narratives. In particular, we tested the accessibility
  of these omitted memories and explored the reasons for omission from life narratives.
  Younger age of memories but not valence predicted omission. Further, omitted memories
  could be remembered when aided with memory prompts. Both remembering and including
  life events in the current life narrative appeared to be related to personal relevance
  at the time of telling, supporting prior research that emphasises the role of motivation,
  ongoing goals, and concerns for the construction of personal narratives.
