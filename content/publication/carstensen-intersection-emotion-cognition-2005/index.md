abstract: 'Divergent trajectories characterize the aging mind: Processing capacity
  declines, while judgment, knowledge, and emotion regulation are relatively spared.
  We maintain that these different developmental trajectories have implications for
  emotion--cognition interactions. Following an overview of our theoretical position,
  we review empirical studies indicating that (a) older adults evidence superior cognitive
  performance for emotional relative to non-emotional information, (b) age differences
  are most evident when the emotional content is positively as opposed to negatively
  valenced, and (c) differences can be accounted for by changes in motivation posited
  in socioemotional selectivity theory.'
