abstract: Two studies explored the role of implicit theories of intelligence in adolescents'
  mathematics achievement. In Study 1 with 373 7th graders, the belief that intelligence
  is malleable (incremental theory) predicted an upward trajectory in grades over
  the two years of junior high school, while a belief that intelligence is fixed (entity
  theory) predicted a flat trajectory. A mediational model including learning goals,
  positive beliefs about effort, and causal attributions and strategies was tested.
  In Study 2, an intervention teaching an incremental theory to 7th graders (               N               =48)
  promoted positive change in classroom motivation, compared with a control group
  (               N               =43). Simultaneously, students in the control group
  displayed a continuing downward trajectory in grades, while this decline was reversed
  for students in the experimental group.
