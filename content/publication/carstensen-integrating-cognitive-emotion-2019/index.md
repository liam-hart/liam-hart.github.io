abstract: Thirty years ago, the subfields of emotion and cognition operated relatively
  independently and the associated science reflected the tacit view that they were
  distinct constructs. Today, questions about the integration of cognition and emotion
  are among the most interesting questions in the field. I offer a personal view of
  the key changes that fuelled this shift over time and describe research from my
  group that unfolded in parallel and led to the identification of the positivity
  effect.
